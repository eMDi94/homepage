Scala is the first functional language that I learned when I started working at my current company.

I first encountered it during my years in University, but I wasn't amazed by it. I thought that it was too difficult
to learn.

I still tought like that back when I first entered my current working place. But then, also thanks to a good friend, I gave it a
shot. At first it was really difficult, but then I remembered how I wasn't feeling comfortable with OOP, so I tried to take as
a learning experience to learn functional programming and shifting my mindset.

Wow. It took some time, but I fell in love with both the language and functional programming.
The functional constructs are very beatiful, sometimes I find myself thinking that maybe it was even better to push a little
more toward the functional world. That's why when **[ZIO](https://zio.dev)** came out I fell even more in love with the language.
Functional Programming at its finest. A dream come true.

I can't say that I am a master of functional programming or Scala, but whenever I have the time, I try to learn some more about it and its ecosystem.

On the working environment we are shifting to other languages and gradually abandoning it, but in the future I hope to use it again at its full power.
